# Barangay Processes Knowledge Base

## Overview

Barangays are the smallest administrative and political units in the Philippines, established under the Local Government Code of 1991 (Republic Act No. 7160). Each barangay is headed by a Barangay Captain (Punong Barangay) and is supported by Barangay Councilors (Sangguniang Barangay members), a Barangay Secretary, and a Barangay Treasurer.

Barangays serve as the frontline of government service delivery in the Philippines. They are responsible for maintaining peace and order within their jurisdiction, facilitating community-level dispute resolution, issuing official certifications and clearances, implementing local ordinances, and coordinating with city or municipal governments on matters of public welfare.

Residents of a barangay are entitled to access various services and documents that are essential for employment, business, legal transactions, and social assistance. These services are generally affordable, accessible, and designed to be processed within the barangay hall without requiring residents to travel to city or municipal offices.

## 1. Barangay Clearance

### Description

A Barangay Clearance is an official document issued by the Punong Barangay (Barangay Captain) certifying that the requesting individual has no pending criminal, civil, or administrative case within the barangay's jurisdiction. It serves as proof of good standing within the community and is one of the most commonly requested documents from the barangay.

Barangay Clearances are typically required for employment applications, opening bank accounts, applying for government-issued IDs, enrolling in schools or universities, applying for loans or financial assistance, and other official transactions that require proof of character and community standing.

The clearance is valid for a period typically ranging from six months to one year from the date of issuance, depending on the barangay's local policies. Residents are advised to check the validity period printed on their clearance before submitting it to any institution.

### Who Can Apply

Any person who is a registered resident of the barangay may apply for a Barangay Clearance. Applicants must have resided in the barangay for a minimum period, typically at least six months, though this may vary per barangay. Non-residents who work or conduct business within the barangay may also be eligible in some cases.

### Requirements

- One (1) valid government-issued ID (e.g., PhilSys ID, Voter's ID, Driver's License, Passport, SSS/GSIS ID, or Postal ID)
- Proof of residency (e.g., utility bill, lease contract, or barangay-issued certificate of residency)
- Accomplished application form (available at the barangay hall)
- Recent 1x1 or 2x2 ID photo (some barangays require this)
- Community Tax Certificate (Cedula), which can also be obtained at the barangay hall

### Steps

1. Proceed to the barangay hall during office hours, typically 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM on weekdays.
2. Secure and fill out the application form at the barangay secretary's office.
3. Submit the completed form along with all required documents for verification.
4. Pay the required processing fee at the barangay treasurer's window and obtain an official receipt.
5. Wait for the barangay staff to process and prepare the clearance document.
6. The Barangay Captain or an authorized official signs and seals the clearance.
7. Receive the official Barangay Clearance document.

### Processing Time

Processing is typically completed within 10 to 30 minutes for walk-in applicants, provided all requirements are complete. During peak periods such as the start of the school year or local government deadlines, processing may take longer. Some barangays offer same-day release while others may require return the following day.

### Fees

The fee for a Barangay Clearance typically ranges from PHP 20 to PHP 100, depending on the barangay's local revenue ordinance. Some barangays also charge an additional fee for the Community Tax Certificate (Cedula) if it is processed simultaneously. Indigent residents or senior citizens may be entitled to reduced fees or exemptions depending on local policy.

### Common Uses

- Pre-employment clearance submission to employers
- Business permit application at the city or municipal level
- Bank account opening requirements
- NBI Clearance application supporting document
- Scholarship application requirements
- Government financial assistance applications

## 2. Certificate of Indigency

### Description

A Certificate of Indigency is an official document issued by the barangay certifying that a resident belongs to a low-income household and is financially incapable of paying for certain services, medical treatments, legal assistance, or educational fees. It is commonly used to access free or subsidized services from government agencies, hospitals, and non-governmental organizations.

The certificate is typically based on the barangay's knowledge of the applicant's socioeconomic status within the community. The Barangay Captain or authorized official verifies the applicant's financial condition before issuing the certificate, often through interviews or cross-referencing with the barangay's list of indigent residents.

This document is particularly important for residents seeking access to the Malasakit Center program, PhilHealth indigent coverage, assistance from the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), legal aid services from the Public Attorney's Office (PAO), and scholarship programs for financially disadvantaged students.

### Who Can Apply

Any resident who is classified as belonging to the lowest income bracket or who is unable to meet basic financial needs may apply for a Certificate of Indigency. Priority is typically given to individuals who are unemployed, elderly, persons with disabilities, solo parents, or those facing a medical emergency.

### Requirements

- One (1) valid government-issued ID
- Proof of residency such as a utility bill or lease agreement
- Optional: Request or referral letter from the institution requiring the certificate (e.g., hospital, school, or government agency)
- Optional: Supporting documents demonstrating financial need, such as a certificate of unemployment or a medical abstract in cases of medical assistance applications

### Steps

1. Visit the barangay hall and inform the barangay staff of the purpose for requesting the Certificate of Indigency.
2. Fill out the required request or application form.
3. Provide any supporting documents or explain the circumstances to the barangay official.
4. The barangay may conduct a brief interview or verification with the applicant.
5. Once verified, the barangay secretary prepares the certificate.
6. The Barangay Captain signs and affixes the official barangay seal.
7. The certificate is released to the applicant, usually on the same day.

### Processing Time

The Certificate of Indigency is typically processed and released on the same day of application, provided the applicant is present and the required documents are available. Urgent cases, such as those involving hospitalized patients, are often prioritized.

### Fees

The Certificate of Indigency is generally issued free of charge or at a minimal administrative fee. This reflects the document's purpose of assisting financially disadvantaged individuals. Some barangays may charge a nominal fee for the documentary stamp, but this is usually waived for qualifying applicants.

### Common Uses

- Hospital admission assistance and free medical services
- PhilHealth indigent membership enrollment
- DSWD assistance applications such as Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situation (AICS)
- Public Attorney's Office (PAO) legal aid eligibility
- Scholarship and educational assistance programs
- Malasakit Center referrals for medical financial assistance

## 3. Barangay Business Clearance

### Description

A Barangay Business Clearance is an official document certifying that a business establishment operating within the barangay complies with local barangay regulations, ordinances, and community standards. It is one of the primary requirements for obtaining a Business Permit from the city or municipal government and must be secured before any business can legally operate within the barangay's jurisdiction.

The clearance signifies that the barangay has no objection to the establishment and operation of the business within its territory. It also serves as confirmation that the business location has been reviewed and is not in violation of any zoning regulations enforced at the barangay level.

Both new businesses and existing businesses undergoing annual renewal are required to secure this clearance. Failure to obtain a Barangay Business Clearance can result in the denial of the city or municipal business permit and may expose the business owner to penalties under local ordinance.

### Who Can Apply

Any individual, sole proprietor, partnership, corporation, or cooperative intending to establish or renew a business within the barangay must apply for a Barangay Business Clearance. The applicant may be the business owner, a duly authorized representative, or the designated officer of a company.

### Requirements

**For New Businesses:**
- Accomplished application form
- Valid government-issued ID of the business owner or authorized representative
- DTI Certificate of Business Name Registration (for sole proprietors) or SEC Certificate of Incorporation (for corporations or partnerships)
- Lease contract or proof of ownership of the business location
- Sketch or vicinity map of the business location
- Community Tax Certificate (Cedula) of the business owner

**For Business Renewal:**
- Previous Barangay Business Clearance
- Previous year's Business Permit from the city or municipal government
- Valid ID of the owner or authorized representative
- Updated lease contract if applicable

### Steps

1. Obtain and accomplish the Business Clearance application form at the barangay hall.
2. Submit the completed form along with all required documents to the barangay secretary.
3. The barangay staff reviews the submitted documents for completeness and accuracy.
4. A barangay official may conduct an on-site inspection of the business premises if required, particularly for new businesses or those with potential community impact.
5. Once inspection and verification are completed, the applicant pays the applicable clearance fee at the treasurer's window.
6. The Barangay Captain or authorized official signs the clearance.
7. The applicant receives the Barangay Business Clearance.

### Processing Time

Processing typically takes one to three working days, depending on whether an inspection is required and the completeness of submitted documents. During the business permit renewal season, which usually falls in January of each year, processing times may be longer due to high volume of applications.

### Fees

Fees for Barangay Business Clearances vary depending on the type and size of the business. Micro and small businesses may be charged PHP 300 to PHP 1,000, while medium to large businesses may be charged higher amounts. Fees are set through local revenue ordinances and may be updated annually.

### Common Uses

- Primary requirement for obtaining or renewing a Business Permit at the city or municipal hall
- Proof of barangay-level compliance for business operations
- Supporting document for bank loans or business financing applications
- Submission to regulatory agencies such as BIR for registration

## 4. Blotter Report

### Description

A Blotter Report is an official record of an incident, complaint, or dispute that has been formally reported to and recorded by the barangay. The term "blotter" refers to the logbook maintained by the barangay in which all reported incidents are chronologically entered. This practice follows the same concept used by the Philippine National Police (PNP) in maintaining police blotters.

Filing a blotter report at the barangay level is typically the first step in documenting a complaint or incident before it escalates to higher authorities such as the police or the courts. It establishes an official record of when and where the incident was reported and provides the complainant with a document that can be used in subsequent legal proceedings if necessary.

Common incidents recorded in the barangay blotter include neighbor disputes, threats, minor physical altercations, theft, property damage, noise complaints, domestic disturbances, and other community-level conflicts. More serious criminal matters such as serious physical injury, robbery, or homicide are typically referred directly to the Philippine National Police.

### Who Can File

Any resident, visitor, or person who has experienced or witnessed an incident within the barangay's jurisdiction may file a blotter report. The complainant does not need to be a registered resident of the barangay to file a report if the incident occurred within the barangay's territory.

### Requirements

- Valid government-issued ID of the complainant
- Written or verbal personal statement describing the incident
- Names and addresses of involved parties, if known
- Supporting documents or evidence, if available (e.g., photos, screenshots of messages, medical certificate for injuries)

### Steps

1. Visit the barangay hall and inform the duty officer or barangay tanod that you wish to file a blotter report.
2. Provide a detailed account of the incident including the date, time, location, and persons involved.
3. The barangay secretary or duty officer records the incident in the official blotter logbook.
4. The entry includes the complainant's name, the nature of the incident, parties involved, and the action taken or recommended.
5. The complainant may request a certified true copy of the blotter entry for their personal records or for submission to other authorities.
6. The barangay may recommend mediation, refer the matter to the Lupon Tagapamayapa, or endorse it to the police depending on the nature and severity of the incident.

### Processing Time

Recording of a blotter report is done immediately upon the complainant's visit. The actual time spent at the barangay hall typically ranges from 15 to 45 minutes depending on the complexity of the account and the availability of barangay staff.

### Fees

Filing a blotter report is generally free of charge. If the complainant requests a certified true copy of the blotter entry, a minimal fee for documentary processing may apply, usually ranging from PHP 20 to PHP 50.

### Important Notes

A barangay blotter entry is not the same as a formal criminal complaint. It is a record of a reported incident and does not automatically result in arrest or prosecution. However, it serves as crucial documentation in legal proceedings and can support future complaints filed with the police or the courts.

## 5. Barangay Dispute Resolution (Lupon Tagapamayapa)

### Description

The Lupon Tagapamayapa, which translates to "Committee for the Restoration of Peace," is a body established under the Katarungang Pambarangay Law (Presidential Decree No. 1508, later incorporated into the Local Government Code of 1991). It is composed of barangay residents appointed by the Barangay Captain to serve as mediators and conciliators in community disputes.

The Lupon Tagapamayapa handles the amicable settlement of disputes between residents through mediation and conciliation proceedings. The objective is to resolve conflicts at the community level without resorting to costly and time-consuming litigation. In many types of civil and criminal cases where the parties involved are residents of the same barangay or neighboring barangays, the law requires that barangay conciliation be attempted before the case can be filed in court.

Disputes commonly handled by the Lupon Tagapamayapa include neighbor conflicts over property boundaries, noise, and access rights; family disputes not covered by family courts; minor physical injuries; damage to property; collection of debts below a certain amount; and other civil matters between individuals residing within the same barangay or within different barangays of the same city or municipality.

### Composition

- **Punong Barangay (Barangay Captain):** Serves as the ex officio Lupon Chairperson and presides over initial conciliation proceedings.
- **Lupon Members:** Appointed residents known for their integrity, impartiality, and credibility in the community. The Lupon has a minimum of 10 and a maximum of 20 members.
- **Pangkat Tagapagkasundo (Conciliation Panel):** A three-member panel selected from the Lupon to handle specific cases during the hearing stage.

### Who Can File

Any resident who has a dispute with another resident of the same or neighboring barangay within the same city or municipality may file a complaint with the Lupon Tagapamayapa. Under the Katarungang Pambarangay Law, certain cases must first undergo barangay conciliation before being filed in court.

### Steps

1. The complainant files a written or verbal complaint at the barangay hall, addressed to the Lupon Tagapamayapa.
2. The Barangay Captain summons both parties and attempts to mediate the dispute within 15 days from the date of filing.
3. If mediation by the Barangay Captain fails, the case is referred to the Pangkat Tagapagkasundo.
4. The Pangkat schedules conciliation hearings and both parties are notified of the schedule.
5. Both parties attend the conciliation meetings and present their respective accounts.
6. The Pangkat facilitates discussion and encourages both parties to arrive at an amicable settlement.
7. If an agreement is reached, a settlement document (Kasunduang Pag-aayos) is signed by both parties and attested by the Barangay Captain.
8. If no settlement is reached within the prescribed period, a Certificate to File Action (CFA) is issued to the complainant, allowing them to bring the matter to court.

### Possible Outcomes

- **Amicable Settlement (Kasunduang Pag-aayos):** Both parties agree to terms of resolution. The settlement document has the force and effect of a final judgment of a court once approved by the Lupon.
- **Repudiation:** Either party may repudiate the settlement within 10 days of signing by filing a sworn statement with the Barangay Captain.
- **Certificate to File Action (CFA):** Issued when no settlement is reached after the conciliation period, allowing the complainant to escalate the matter to the appropriate court or government agency.

### Processing Time

The Katarungang Pambarangay Law sets a maximum conciliation period. The Barangay Captain has 15 days to conduct mediation. If the case is referred to the Pangkat, it has 15 days to conciliate, extendable by another 15 days upon agreement of both parties. The entire process at the barangay level may take from a few days to over a month depending on the complexity of the dispute and the cooperation of both parties.

### Fees

Barangay conciliation proceedings are free of charge. There are no filing fees for bringing a complaint before the Lupon Tagapamayapa.

### Cases Exempt from Barangay Conciliation

Certain cases are not required to undergo barangay conciliation before being filed in court. These include cases involving parties who do not reside in the same city or municipality, criminal offenses with penalties of imprisonment exceeding one year or a fine exceeding PHP 5,000, cases involving government units or officials in their official capacity, and cases where immediate urgent legal relief is necessary.

## 6. Certificate of Residency

### Description

A Certificate of Residency is an official document issued by the barangay certifying that a specific individual is a bona fide resident of the barangay. It confirms the person's name, address, and the duration of their residency within the barangay's jurisdiction. This document is one of the most frequently requested barangay certifications and is used across a wide variety of official and personal transactions.

The certificate is based on the barangay's official records of registered residents and may be verified through the barangay's list of households or voter registration records. Barangay staff may conduct verification through interviews with barangay tanods or neighboring residents in cases where the applicant's residency record is not immediately on file.

### Who Can Apply

Any individual who lives within the barangay and can demonstrate proof of their residence may apply for a Certificate of Residency. This includes property owners, long-term tenants, and family members who share a household within the barangay's territory.

### Requirements

- One (1) valid government-issued ID showing the applicant's name
- Proof of residency such as a recent utility bill (electricity, water, or internet) issued within the last three months, a lease contract, a deed of sale of property, or a bank statement showing the applicant's address
- Community Tax Certificate (Cedula), which can be processed at the barangay hall

### Steps

1. Visit the barangay hall during regular office hours and inform the staff of the purpose for requesting the certificate.
2. Submit the required identification and proof of residency documents.
3. The barangay secretary verifies the applicant's details against the barangay's resident records.
4. If necessary, a barangay tanod or official may be asked to confirm the applicant's residence.
5. The barangay secretary prepares the Certificate of Residency.
6. The Barangay Captain or authorized official signs the certificate and affixes the official barangay seal.
7. The applicant receives the document, usually on the same day.

### Processing Time

Processing is typically completed within the same day of application, usually within 15 to 30 minutes, provided all requirements are in order and the barangay's records confirm the applicant's residency.

### Fees

The fee for a Certificate of Residency is generally the same as that of a Barangay Clearance, ranging from PHP 20 to PHP 100 depending on the barangay's local revenue ordinance. Some barangays issue it at no cost for applicants who require it for government assistance programs.

### Common Uses

- School enrollment or transfer requirements
- Scholarship application supporting document
- Government ID application requirements such as PhilSys, Voter's ID, or Driver's License
- Bank account opening
- Voter registration or transfer of voting precinct
- Social welfare program applications including 4Ps (Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program)
- Employment application supporting documents

## 7. Barangay ID

### Description

A Barangay ID is an identification card issued by the barangay to its registered residents. While it is not a nationally recognized primary ID, it serves as a valid proof of identity and residency within the barangay and is accepted in many local transactions, particularly for residents who do not yet possess a government-issued ID.

The Barangay ID typically contains the resident's full name, address, photo, date of birth, barangay and address, signature, and the signature of the Barangay Captain. Some barangays also include emergency contact information. The card is laminated and issued as a wallet-sized document.

### Requirements

- Accomplished application form
- Recent 1x1 or 2x2 ID photo
- Proof of residency
- Birth certificate (for minors)

### Steps

1. Visit the barangay hall and request an application form for a Barangay ID.
2. Fill out the form and attach the required photo and documents.
3. Submit the completed application to the barangay secretary.
4. The barangay prepares and prints the ID card.
5. Upon completion, the Barangay Captain signs the ID.
6. The applicant receives the Barangay ID.

### Processing Time

Processing time varies depending on the barangay's printing and issuance process, typically ranging from one to three days. Some barangays with digital printing capabilities may issue the ID on the same day.

### Fees

Fees for a Barangay ID typically range from PHP 50 to PHP 150, covering the cost of printing and lamination.

## 8. Solo Parent Certificate

### Description

A Solo Parent Certificate is issued by the barangay to certify that a resident qualifies as a solo parent under Republic Act No. 8972, also known as the Solo Parents' Welfare Act of 2000. This certificate is required when applying for the Solo Parent ID from the city or municipal social welfare office, which entitles the holder to a range of government benefits and privileges.

Solo parents are individuals who are left alone with the responsibility of parenthood due to the absence of a spouse caused by death, abandonment, separation, or other circumstances. The barangay certificate serves as an initial certification before the applicant proceeds to the City or Municipal Social Welfare and Development Office (C/MSWDO) for the official Solo Parent ID.

### Requirements

- Valid government-issued ID
- Proof of residency
- Birth certificate of the child or children
- Death certificate of spouse (if applicable)
- Affidavit of abandonment or separation (if applicable)
- Any supporting documents demonstrating solo parent status

### Steps

1. Visit the barangay hall and explain the purpose of the request.
2. Submit all required documents for review by the barangay social worker or secretary.
3. The barangay verifies the applicant's situation through document review and may conduct a home visit.
4. Once verified, the Barangay Captain issues a certification of solo parent status.
5. The applicant proceeds to the C/MSWDO for processing of the official Solo Parent ID.

### Processing Time

Processing at the barangay level typically takes one to three days depending on verification requirements.

### Benefits of the Solo Parent ID

- 10% discount on baby products, medicines, and other essential goods
- Parental leave benefits of up to seven days per year
- Priority in government housing programs
- Access to livelihood development programs
- Educational benefits for the children of solo parents

## General Information

### Barangay Hall Office Hours

Most barangay halls operate Monday through Friday from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Some barangays may offer extended hours or Saturday services depending on demand. Residents are advised to visit during regular office hours to ensure that the Barangay Captain and authorized signatories are available.

### Community Tax Certificate (Cedula)

The Community Tax Certificate, commonly referred to as Cedula, is required for most barangay transactions. It can be obtained at the barangay hall or the city/municipal treasury. Fees for the Cedula are based on income and real property ownership. For individuals with no taxable income, the basic community tax is PHP 5.00.

### Barangay Hotlines and Assistance

Residents may contact their barangay for emergency assistance, community concerns, or inquiries about services. Many barangays now maintain Facebook pages and group chats for faster communication with residents. Emergency situations such as fires, floods, or medical emergencies should be reported to both the barangay and the appropriate emergency services.

### Anti-Fixers Policy

Residents are advised to transact directly with barangay staff only and to avoid fixers who may offer to process documents for a fee. Barangay services are generally affordable and accessible, and residents should report any suspicious activity or extortion attempts to the Barangay Captain or the appropriate authorities.
